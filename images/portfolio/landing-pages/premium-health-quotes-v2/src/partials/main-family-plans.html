Building security by providing you and your loved ones with the protection and coverage you need is what health insurance is all about. Today’s insurance plans cover essential health benefits and offer a wide range of consumer protections (and protection from escalating health care and drug costs).

Health insurance may be confusing and intimidating, what with subsidies, penalties, qualifying events, special enrollments. The reality, though, is that getting health insurance is increasingly becoming easier to get. Gone are the days where insurance companies could charge more for or deny coverage due to a pre-existing condition. The same goes for insurance companies being able to impose limitations or caps on the maximum coverage allowed for a specific illness, condition, or care.

There are many types of plans to choose from (HMO, PPO, POS, etc.), each with unique features. Before you make any decisions concerning your health and health insurance, you should do your due diligence by gathering as much information as possible about your household income, the plans and their features, and assess your health care and prescription medication needs. Review the plans to learn if your current doctors, hospitals, and pharmacy are in the network. Then review the plans for deductibles, premiums, and out-pocket maximums and copayments. Then talk with a licensed insurance professional who can help you make an informed buying decision to maximize your benefits and save you time and money.
