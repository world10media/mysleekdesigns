Health Insurance is Too Important to Go Without.

Most people are interested in saving money when possible. It’s no different when it comes to health insurance and health care. We want the best available but we don’t necessarily want, nor can we afford, high rates on our health insurance or expensive medical care.

Medical care and prescription drug costs are climbing and there’s little chance that’s going to stop anytime soon. So while we may not have any ability to control those things, we do have ways we can lower our share of those costs and the impact they have on our finances.

Here are few ways you reduce what you pay for health insurance:

  • You’re losing or recently lost your health coverage through a life event
    • You lost your job, you lost your employer-sponsored health insurance, you got a divorce or are getting divorced, your COBRA coverage expired, you’re no longer eligible for Medicaid or CHIP, or your current health plan was decertified
  • You’re having or adopting a child, or placing a child up for adoption
  • You’re getting married (or just got married)
  • You’re moving to another part of the state or country and your insurance is not available there
  • You were unable to enroll due to some error when enrolling
  • You’re graduating from college and losing your school’s health plan
  • You’ve had a change in income or household status that changes your eligibility for tax credits or subsidies
  • You became a legal U.S. resident or citizen

Consult a licensed insurance professional to learn more about ways you can lower your health insurance costs and get a plan that works for you.
